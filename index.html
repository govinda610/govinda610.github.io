I started with economics — studying how automation reshapes labour markets in countries like India, where the stakes are highest.

So I stopped studying the impact of the machine, and started building it.

At Citigroup I did ML and forecasting. At Eli Lilly I compressed days of data science into minutes. At Gartner I orchestrate fleets of agents that reason, retrieve and act.

But applications aren't the destination. I want to work on the architecture of intelligence itself — interpretability, training dynamics, new attention mechanisms, world models, VLAs. The long game: a deep-tech company that matters, or a quant fund with a systematic market edge. The Jim Simons model.
